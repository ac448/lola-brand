LOLA

An AI project manager for creative teams. She reads work requests and routes them on her own. Any call can be undone in one click.

A build and execution through Technovation

The problem

Every creative team has a doorway problem.

Requests arrive from everywhere. Forms, DMs, hallway asks, the dreaded "quick favor." Someone has to read each one, figure out what it actually is, find the right owner, and set a deadline before any creative work can start. On a global marketing team, that triage job quietly eats hours every week and the person doing it is usually the same person the work is waiting on.

LOLA takes the reading and matching off that person's plate. Every call she makes can be undone.

The mark

A letter with a face inside it.

One shape, two jobs

The L is the lettermark and the frame at the same time. It carries the warm gradient and gives the face somewhere to sit. Nothing in the mark is decorative.

The face is the intelligence

Shaped like a chat bubble because that is the honest form. You talk to her, she answers. The cool gradient belongs to output.

The bubble sits inside the letter

Nothing floats. The structure contains the personality, which is exactly how the product is built. The mark says the same thing the architecture does.

The field is where she lives

LOLA Blue is the environment. The glow rises from below, warm and ambient, signaling activity without alarm. The mark never sits on white by default.

The system

Warm acts. Cool confirms.

The palette is functional. Warm tones belong to anything LOLA does on her own. Cool tones appear after a person confirms her call. Cream marks the moments she steps back and asks for help. Color carries state across every surface, from the logo to a status chip in a task list.

Architecture

Five steps. No waiting.

A request comes in

A structured work request form, inside the project management tool the team already uses. The intake lives where the work lives.

LOLA reads and reasons

The form hits a webhook. LOLA sends the contents to Claude through the Anthropic API alongside the team's routing rulebook, a plain-language document the team owns and edits.

She routes and shows her work

Owner, project, priority, and the exact rule that produced the decision: matched: rule 2.1 (event collateral → design queue). Every call is auditable because every call cites its source.

?

No matching rule? She stops.

Requests that fall outside the rulebook get flagged to the triage owner with a note about what was missing. Guessing is not in her permission set.

Work moves. A human can undo it.

The task is assigned the moment she routes it. The triage owner can reverse or edit any call in one click. A reviewed call turns cool.

Anthropic API · Node.js · Vercel · Asana (first integration)

REVERSIBLE

Reversibility is the design decision.

LOLA routes work the moment a rule matches. Nothing she does is final. Every call carries a one-click undo and a citation back to the rule that produced it. Autonomy is safe when the team can take any decision back.
