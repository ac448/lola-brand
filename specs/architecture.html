LOLA · Product Overview · Spec 02

How LOLA works

LOLA is an AI routing agent for creative and marketing teams. She reads each request on the team's Asana board and matches it against your team's own routing rules. Today she proposes every call and a human approves it. Live mode, where she assigns the work herself, is the gated next step. Every call she makes cites the rule behind it.

Request lifecycle

1

A request lands on the board

Anyone adds a task to the team's Asana board. A webhook wakes LOLA. She verifies the signature before she trusts the event, then reads the task. An unverified caller is rejected with a 401.

2

She routes it

If the Deliverable Type dropdown is set, LOLA looks the owner up in a fixed table, deterministic and free. If it is blank, Claude Sonnet reads the free text and infers the owner. She posts her reasoning as a comment and cites the rule, e.g. matched: rule 2.1 (event collateral → design queue).

state → ROUTED (warm chip)

3

She checks the brief

Claude Opus reads each owner-routed request and flags a brief too thin to start on. When a Google Drive account is connected, LOLA checks that linked files open and asks the requester to share the ones that do not.

?

Unclear? She hands it up.

A multi-format campaign or an unclear request goes to the triage owner and moves to Pending Info. A flagged sender is escalated the same way. LOLA notes what was missing. Guessing is not in her permission set.

state → NEEDS HUMAN (cream chip)

4

She acts, gated

In safety mode LOLA comments her call and tags the triage owner, writing nothing to the board. In live mode she assigns the owner, sets the due date from the form, and moves the card to Assigned. Live mode needs two separate switches, so a single deploy can never start her writing by accident. Once a person reviews a call and keeps it, the chip turns cool.

state → REVIEWED (cool chip)

The stack

Intelligence

Anthropic API

Claude Sonnet routes when the dropdown is blank. Claude Opus checks each brief for completeness.

Runtime

Node.js

One serverless function verifies the webhook, orchestrates the round trip, and comments back.

Hosting

Vercel

Serverless deploy. No infrastructure for a creative team to babysit.

Workspace

Asana, today

First integration. The routing table is tool-agnostic by design.

Files

Google Drive

A service account checks that linked files open before work starts. Optional, added in Phase 2.

Design principles

Gated before autonomous

LOLA shipped proposing every call and writing nothing. Live mode takes two separate switches and a week of proof. A kill switch stops her in one step.

The rulebook belongs to the team

Routing logic lives in a plain-language document and the team edits it directly. Changing how work flows is a writing task. No engineering ticket required.

Every decision cites a rule

No black-box assignments. If LOLA can't point to the rule behind a call, she doesn't make the call.

Color carries state

Warm means LOLA acted. Cool means a person reviewed it. Cream means a human is needed. The interface tells the truth at a glance.
