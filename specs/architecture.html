LOLA · Product Overview · Spec 02

How LOLA works

LOLA is an AI routing agent for creative and marketing teams. She reads each incoming request and matches it against your team's own routing rules. When a rule fits, she assigns the work herself. Every call she makes can be undone in one click.

Request lifecycle

1

A request comes in

Anyone on the team submits a structured work request form inside the project management tool. No new software to learn, the intake lives where the work already lives.

2

LOLA reads and reasons

The request hits a webhook. LOLA sends the form contents to Claude via the Anthropic API alongside the team's routing rulebook, a plain-language document the team owns and can edit. She identifies the request type, the right owner, and the deadline risk.

3

She routes, and shows her work

LOLA assigns the task and posts her reasoning as a comment: owner, project, priority, and the specific rule she used to get there, e.g. matched: rule 2.1 (event collateral → design queue). Every call is auditable because every call cites its source.

state → ROUTED (warm chip)

?

No matching rule? She stops.

If a request doesn't map cleanly to the rulebook, she flags it for the triage owner with a note about what was missing. Guessing is not in her permission set.

state → NEEDS HUMAN (cream chip)

4

A human can undo any call.

Work moves the moment she routes it. The triage owner can reverse or edit any call in one click. Once a person reviews the call and keeps it, the chip turns cool.

state → CONFIRMED (cool chip)

The stack

Intelligence

Anthropic API

Claude handles classification and routing logic against the team rulebook.

Runtime

Node.js

Lightweight service receiving webhooks and orchestrating the round trip.

Hosting

Vercel

Serverless deploy. No infrastructure for a creative team to babysit.

Workspace

Asana, today

First integration. The routing engine is tool-agnostic by design.

Design principles

Autonomous with an undo

LOLA routes without waiting for permission. Nothing she does is final. The triage owner can take back any call in one click.

The rulebook belongs to the team

Routing logic lives in a plain-language document and the team edits it directly. Changing how work flows is a writing task. No engineering ticket required.

Every decision cites a rule

No black-box assignments. If LOLA can't point to the rule behind a call, she doesn't make the call.

Color carries state

Warm means LOLA acted. Cool means a person confirmed it. Cream means a human is needed. The interface tells the truth at a glance.
